Old work / 2018

UBS, TechView.

Working within the credit risk analytical department at UBS, I used UX and visual design to create a mobile app from scratch for internal usage in UBS investment bank.

  • Enterprise UX
  • Mobile
  • B2B

The team

1x UX designer, 1x UX visual designer, 3x front-end developers, 1x Scrum master, 1x business analyst. I was contracting on behalf of Norman and Sons, a digital transformation consultancy specialising in human-centred design.

Approach

We used a user-centric approach to empathise with specific users and extract their needs and pain points, then created many ideas and prototypes to try to solve their issues smartly.

Step 1

Primary research.

It's impossible to design a high-quality product without analysing previous releases and doing in-depth design research.

Step 2

Analyse requirements and user needs.

Analyse key points from sessions with key stakeholders and target users. Prioritise deliverables for future releases.

Step 3

Ideate, sketch, share.

Prototyping is a crucial part of the design process. I used rapid prototyping to get quick feedback from stakeholders and target users on proposed design solutions. Following this approach, I could incorporate changes instantly and reduce the number of future iterations.

Step 4

Iterate.

Iterate and present back amended, more sophisticated designs. Each iteration turned low-fidelity mockups into more thoughtful and holistic design products.

Step 5

Finalise.

Unify design patterns, finalise the design system, prepare screens to be used by the development and further support team.
